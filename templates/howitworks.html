How does Recon work?

Section 1: How do I use Recon?

In the recommended section, cards are sorted by Recon score, from lowest to highest. The lower the Recon score, the better that day is for hiking.

Maximum Temperature


The maximum temperature describes the peak temperature of the day. Typically a temperature of more than 25 degrees celsius is uncomfortable. Room temperature is conventionally 20-22°C

Morning Temperature


The morning temperature serves as an approximate of the lowest temperature that day. It is useful for getting a sense of the range of temperature throughout the day. It's also useful if you're planning to hike in the morning.

Cloudiness


Cloudiness is shown as a percentage of coverage in the sky by clouds. While having more clouds may be more comfortable to walk outside in, it is important to note that while they do reduce some of the sun's UV rays, your skin can still be damaged.

Probability of Precipitation


This shows the chance of precipitation, such as rain, snow, hail, etc.

This Week shows the weather from the current day to next week. Each bar can be clicked on for more details. The Recon score is displayed as the right-most number.

Section 2: How is Recon score calculated?


Weather data is pulled from the Open Weather Map One Call API, which gives daily forecasts for 7 days. Although the Recon website only displays max. temperature, morning temperature, probability of precipitation, and cloudiness, the score takes into consideration other variables like the dew point and UV index, which are less intuitive. The person who made this website is not, in fact, a meteorologist, but instead took 30 minutes to search up some stuff on Google about which values of each weather characteristic create the most comfortable hiking days, so if you have different tastes in weather, or are an actual meteorologist, the Preferences section is made for you!
